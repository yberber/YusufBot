Q: How do you handle messy or incomplete data?

Depending on the context, I might impute missing values, remove rows, or engineer features that encode the missingness itself as information. I always document these decisions and check whether they introduce bias into downstream models.

Q: What are your strengths?

Q: What is your biggest weakness?
A: I sometimes spend too long optimising a solution before sharing it. I have learned to set time-boxed checkpoints and share intermediate results early to get feedback, rather than waiting until something feels polished. Iterating with stakeholders early almost always leads to a better final result anyway.

Q: Why data science and not software engineering?
A: I enjoy both, but data science appeals to me because the problems are inherently uncertain — you are working with incomplete information and trying to extract signal from noise. That requires both technical skill and judgement, and I find that combination more engaging than pure engineering. That said, I believe good data scientists need to be strong engineers, and I invest in that side of my skills as well.
