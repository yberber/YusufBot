Q: What was the focus of your Master's thesis?
A: My Master's thesis focused on multimodal emotion recognition in conversations. I aimed to identify the predominant emotion of an utterance within a conversation. I fine-tuned the LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct model using two-phase QLoRA with systematic ablations. By combining engineered audio features with retrieval-augmented in-context exemplars (RAG), I achieved State-of-the-Art (SOTA) performance on the MELD and IEMOCAP benchmark datasets.

Q: Tell us about the AI agent you developed for your Bachelor's thesis.
A: For my Bachelor's thesis, I re-engineered the AlphaZero algorithm from Google DeepMind to play International Checkers. I used Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and ResNets, and built a simulation environment to generate synthetic training data via self-play. After about 15 days of self-play and training cycles, the agent played quite well—not superhuman, but highly convincing given the computational resources available.

Q: Have you built any end-to-end LLM applications?
A: Yes, I co-developed RAGMedAssist, an end-to-end LLM-powered medical assistant. It utilizes retrieval-augmented generation pipelines tailored specifically for the medical domain to provide accurate conversational assistance.

Q: What is the Anime Face Generator Pipeline?
A: It is a 2024 project where I built and trained a GAN-based image generation pipeline. I used DCGAN and WGAN-GP with PyTorch to generate images, and combined it with Real-ESRGAN for high-quality image upscaling.
